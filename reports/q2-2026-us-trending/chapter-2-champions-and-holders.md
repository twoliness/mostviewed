# Champions and Holders

## The 25 creators who landed multiple videos in Q2's US trending top 25

*Chapter 2 — Q2 2026 YouTube US Trending Playbook*

---

**Across the 55,939 videos that entered US trending in Q2 2026, only 25 channels landed two or more videos at rank 25 or better. Half of them are Indian-market music and news channels. The single most dominant channel by breadth was Tech Master with 5 top-25 videos over 50 total chart-days — no MrBeast, no Kai Cenat, no established creator in the top three by breadth.**

## What this list actually says

**1. US "trending" is heavily Indian-market.**
14 of the 25 champions are Indian music, news, or vlog channels. If your assumption of "US YouTube trending" is that it looks like the US linguistic and cultural mainstream, this list is your correction. YouTube's `mostPopular?regionCode=US` chart appears to serve the US-based Indian and South Asian diaspora audience at a share disproportionate to the general assumption — Tamil news (Polimer, Mathrubhumi, MediaoneTV, Soundarya), Punjabi music (Prem Dhillon, RSMSA, Sony Music India), Telugu content (Think Music India, T-Series Telugu), plus Hindi and cross-regional.

For content strategy this matters directly: buying "US YouTube trending" placement or targeting "US YouTube audience" reaches an audience whose language and cultural interests are more heterogeneous than the standard demographic slide implies.

**2. Sub count is not predictive of Q2 breadth on the chart.**
Prem Dhillon – Topic has 3,070 subscribers (yes, three thousand — it's a YouTube auto-generated Topic channel) and landed 4 Q2 top-25 videos with a peak of rank 2 across 37 days on chart. HYBE LABELS has 81.7M subscribers and landed 3. The subscriber count column above spans **5 orders of magnitude** (3K to 81M) among channels that all cleared the same "≥2 videos in top-25" bar.

If you're an agency or MCN using subscriber count as a scouting proxy, this quarter's top-of-chart performance is orthogonal to that signal.

**3. The dominant channel is Tech Master, not a household name.**
Tech Master (31.6M subs) landed 5 Q2 top-25 videos with best peak 4 and 50 total chart-days. It's the only channel in Q2 to place five videos in the top 25. There's no obvious Western-media equivalent to Tech Master in the traditional creator media coverage; the channel doesn't get quoted in agency briefs.

**4. MrBeast is not on this list.**
MrBeast released videos in Q2 (Chapter 3 shows him at #2 on the peak-velocity leaderboard). But he placed fewer than 2 Q2-first-seen videos in the global top 25, so he doesn't qualify as a Q2 champion by this metric. This is a portfolio consistency finding, not a comment on any individual video: MrBeast's Q2 was one big hit, not multiple hits.

Compare: Sidemen, Box Of Vengeance, Celine Dept, Foeko, Wanderers Live, Toby Fox all placed 2 videos each with best peak 1. Any of them out-produced MrBeast on the "multiple top-25 hits" metric this quarter.

**5. Music and News are the two categories with the deepest bench.**
Every category in the champions list is either Music (Think Music India, Prem Dhillon, HYBE LABELS, Soundarya, Sony Music India, T-Series Telugu — 6 channels) or News (Polimer, Mathrubhumi, MediaoneTV, Middle East Eye — 4 channels). Plus Tech (Tech Master, Linus Tech Tips), animation/vlog (Zack D. Films, Kind Otter, Lakhneet Vlogs), and entertainment (Sidemen, Box Of Vengeance, Celine Dept, Foeko, Wanderers Live).

Gaming, despite dominating chart *composition* (Chapter 6), has zero channels on the Q2 champions list. Gaming's chart share comes from many different channels with one hit each, not from repeat champions. That's a structural creator-side implication: sponsoring "a Gaming creator" and sponsoring "a Music channel" have very different repeatability profiles.

## What to do with this list

**For MCN / agency scouts:** the four sub-1M-sub channels (Prem Dhillon Topic, "—" listed channels where subscribers weren't returned by the API) place multiple top-25 hits without a corresponding subscriber base. That's a market inefficiency worth reviewing before Q3.

**For content strategists:** if a Q3 quarterly plan does not budget for reaching or partnering with Indian-market creators, the plan is under-representing the US YouTube trending surface by roughly half. The share is not marginal.

**For brand pitches:** when a brand cites "US YouTube reach" targets, ask which of the 25 Q2 champions are on the target list. If the answer is "Sidemen and Celine Dept," they're planning against the ~15% of the champions who match Western-media expectations of "US YouTube."

## Methodology

- **Sample:** 55,939 videos with `first_seen` in `[2026-04-15, 2026-06-27)`, filtered to `peak_rank ≤ 25`.
- **Aggregation:** grouped by `channel_id`, kept groups with ≥ 2 videos. Sorted by (video count DESC, best peak ASC, total days on chart DESC).
- **Subscriber count:** from `creators.subscriber_count` at the most recent 12h creator-refresh tick. Missing = "—" (channel not sampled by the profile-refresh cron before Q2 close).
- **Peak rank source:** `video_summary.peak_rank`, taken across all `video_rank_history` charts including cross-category and country charts. A video's best rank ever, in any chart context.
- **Confidence:** portfolio-level metric is robust to individual-video reconstruction gaps because ranking on the top-25 threshold requires at least one non-null peak per video and a video without any recovered rank_history rows is excluded.
