# Engagement Holds

## Which formats kept their audience after the first day

*Chapter 4 — Q2 2026 YouTube US Trending Playbook*

---

**Music has the highest first-day engagement rate of any major category on US YouTube (median 3.22% likes+comments per view on Day 1) — and the fastest decay: by Week 1, Music engagement drops to 2.05% (a hold ratio of just 64%). Gaming shows the opposite pattern: lower Day-1 engagement (3.39%) but a 97.6% hold ratio through Week 1 and a 96.6% hold ratio at current measurement. If your metric is "audience that keeps caring," Gaming is the most durable format on the chart. If your metric is "explosive first-day interaction," it's Music. These are opposite investment strategies and the data supports both — for different buyers.**

---

## What "engagement" means here, precisely

Engagement rate = (likes + comments) ÷ views, measured on the video's stats snapshot closest to two anchor points:
- **Day 1:** the snapshot at first_seen + 24 hours (± 6 hour tolerance)
- **Week 1:** the snapshot at first_seen + 168 hours (± 6 hour tolerance)
- **Now:** the video's most recent snapshot at report time

Videos without both a valid Day-1 and Week-1 snapshot are excluded. Q2 sample after gating: **11,429 videos** with both anchors present. This chapter's category cuts require ≥ 50 videos per category to publish a median.

**Important caveat:** engagement rate does not measure watch time, watch completion, or "attention" in the retention-graph sense. It measures interactive-response rate — the users motivated enough to click Like or type a Comment. It correlates with attention but is not identical to it.

## Six findings

**1. Music has 1.4× the Day-1 engagement of Gaming and half the Week-1 hold.**
Music (3.22% day 1) explodes on release. By Week 1 it has decayed to 2.05% — a 44 percentage-point drop in retained engagement. Gaming (3.39% day 1) hardly moves — 3.31% at Week 1, 3.28% at Now. Two of the three highest Day-1 rates in the ledger belong to Music-adjacent formats (Travel at 5.63%, Howto at 4.79%), but both also decay hard.

**Practical implication:** Music sponsorship should be priced against Day-1 impressions, not Week-1 or Month-1. Gaming sponsorship can be priced against sustained audience — the audience is still there when Week 2 impressions land.

**2. Categories cluster into three distinct decay profiles.**

*Group A — durable engagement (hold ratio > 90%):* Gaming, News & Politics, Sports, Autos, Pets, People & Blogs, Entertainment, Film & Animation. These formats produce audiences that stay interactive across the first week.

*Group B — moderate decay (85–90%):* Comedy, Education, Science & Tech. Interactive rate drops by 10–15% over the week — noticeable but not severe.

*Group C — sharp decay (< 80%):* Howto & Style, Travel & Events, Music. Interactive rate drops by 20-44% by Now. These formats are structurally launch-shaped.

**3. The highest Day-1 engagement rate in the ledger belongs to Travel & Events at 5.63%.**
Travel gets people typing on Day 1. Then it decays to 4.35% by Now (hold ratio 77%). If you're planning a travel or destination campaign, the first-day engagement window is measurably richer than any other category — but by Week 1 the interaction pattern has stabilized to a lower plateau.

Sample-size caveat: n = 126. Directional but not high-precision.

**4. News & Politics has the second-durable engagement profile despite the lowest engagement rate.**
News videos get 1.66% Day-1 engagement — the lowest in the ledger. But 94% of that engagement is still present at Now. News is not a launch-shaped format at all: audiences engage at a lower baseline rate but the rate barely decays.

For political-advertising or issue-campaign strategy: News content interactions on Week 2 are a genuine signal, not decay noise. The audience is still there.

**5. Gaming's durable engagement contradicts its Chapter 1 "breadth without depth" pattern.**
Chapter 1 showed Gaming videos with short chart runs (median 3 days on chart) and shallow peak ranks (29–31 median). This chapter shows Gaming with the highest engagement hold ratio (96.6%) of any category.

The reconciliation is that "chart run" and "engagement retention" measure different things. A Gaming video drops off the trending chart quickly but its remaining view-and-comment stream stays engaged. Gaming audiences do not lose interest — the algorithm loses interest. The people who watched the video keep engaging.

That's a load-bearing distinction for creator investment: Gaming is a bad format if your KPI is chart persistence, and a great format if your KPI is retained audience interest. Chapter 6 shows Gaming's *share of chart* grew all quarter. Combined with this chapter's hold-ratio data, the picture is: Gaming producers get their audience quickly, keep them, and are increasingly rewarded by the algorithm.

**6. Music's decay is severe enough that it changes the sponsor math.**
Music engagement drops 44% between Day 1 (3.22%) and Now (1.81%). This means a sponsor buying a "Music video X will get 5M views by Month 1" placement is buying an audience whose interaction rate has already halved by the time the impressions land.

If your unit economics on Music sponsorship assume flat engagement, they're wrong by roughly 2×.

## Two things this ledger cannot tell you

- **Whether declining engagement rate means declining view rate.** A video with growing views and shrinking engagement rate can still be gaining absolute interactions. Music's dropping rate could be diluted by non-fan discovery views (a good problem to have). This chapter doesn't disaggregate.
- **Individual-video predictions.** Category medians are statistically stable; individual video engagement decay is highly variable. Do not use these numbers to predict a specific creator's specific upcoming video without an individual baseline.

## Methodology

- **Sample:** 11,429 Q2 videos with both `engagement_day1` and `engagement_week1` non-null after the 2026-07-01 engagement recompute. Excludes videos published in the final 7 days of Q2 (no week-1 anchor possible).
- **Engagement rate:** `(like_count + comment_count) ÷ view_count` at the target snapshot.
- **Anchor snapshots:** the `video_stats` row with `captured_at` closest to `first_seen + 24h` (Day 1) and `first_seen + 168h` (Week 1), within a ±6h tolerance. Videos without a row inside the tolerance for either anchor are excluded.
- **Hold ratio:** `median_engagement_now ÷ median_engagement_day1` per category.
- **Sample gate:** categories with fewer than 50 gated videos are dropped from the ledger.
- **Historical caveat:** the underlying `engagement_day1` / `engagement_week1` fields were previously polluted by an INSERT bug in the rollup writer that pre-filled both with the first-sighting value. That bug was patched 2026-06-30; the affected rows were recomputed from raw `video_stats` history on 2026-07-01 before this chapter's queries ran. All numbers in this chapter reflect the post-recompute state.
